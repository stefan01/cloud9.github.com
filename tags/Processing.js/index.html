The Search Operators 19 Feb 2011

Keyword Input Operator: Surina
Search Operator: Jarek
Lucky Search Operator: Remo

In another parallel Universe, cyber space is not quite the same. Surina the Keyword Input Operator is notorious for her bad work ethic. No one in the Google Web Search Department want to co-operate with her, let alone the customers who often complain about her miserable service. Today is the first day of work for Jarek and Remo the twin brothers who just join the department. They are assigned to work with Surina as a team to serve customersâ€™ search requests. While Jarek functions as the Search Operator, Remo is the Lucky Search Operator. It is a very demanding situation for the twins to perform their duties together with Surina, who yawns uncontrollably and carelessly at work. She constantly produces yawning noises, and pushes the twins away with her wide open mouth. Jarek and Remo wish Surina is only a nightmare, and they will soon wake up and break free of this endless repetition.

Pixel Monster 05 Jan 2011

How many ads and junk information do we have to put up with everyday on the web? Why isn’t there a garbage cleaner at our disposal? I realized that blocking ads is rather passive and there should be “more” to express my feeling for the ads.

I started this experiment with subverting web contents in mind, and chose web images to start. The experiment enables user to send out monsters to clean up images for them, and it’s on Chrome Store now, you can install it here: Pixel Monster.

Yawning Search Bar Demo 23 Nov 2010



UI is the essential element of the web, and I wanted to explore the playful qualities of it.

Why webpages should look the same? Can web space be less virtual by preserving the nuances and qualities that we treasure in the physical world? This Chrome Extension experiment is re-imaging the traditional UI elements in the browser, instead of having the generic looks, UI are shaped with freehand drawing strokes and the search bar is demonstrating the yawing behavior when the user opens the google page.

This experiment is a Chrome Extension which I programmed in javascript and processing.js as drawing library.

Digital Mould 18 Nov 2010

This is a previous project of mine, which I find it closely relates to my thesis direction. It’s an experiment of treating the webpages as a physical space which presents qualities of being able to interact with objects in the environment, in this case objects are the emails. The question opens up by this project, is whether the Web could preserve more qualities or imperfections that we have in the physical world, in order to provide more contexts and ways for people to understand the virtual space and the information that lives in it.
